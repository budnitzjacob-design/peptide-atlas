Peptocopeia Consensus batch prompt
Last updated: 2026-04-21

Use this as one Consensus query per batch of 6-9 peptides. After Consensus returns results, export the result set as CSV or RIS if available. If Consensus gives a chat-style answer, copy the answer plus the visible citation list.

Prompt to paste into Consensus:

For the following peptides: SS-31/elamipretide, MOTS-c, BPC-157, TB-500/thymosin beta-4 fragment, GHK-Cu, tesamorelin, semaglutide, tirzepatide, retatrutide.

Find peer-reviewed evidence and regulatory/clinical-trial sources relevant to:
1. mechanism of action, protein/receptor signaling, genes/proteins, channels/transporters, cytokines/interleukins, and pathway arrows;
2. human clinical evidence including trial phase, population, disease/non-disease context, age range if stated, sample size, route, dose/protocol, duration, endpoints, and adverse events;
3. animal/preclinical evidence with species/model, n if stated, protocol, endpoints, and results;
4. peer-reviewed case reports if any;
5. medical/clinical uses separately from non-clinical enhancement/common-use claims;
6. pharmacokinetics/pharmacodynamics and dosing context, clearly labeled as FDA label, clinical trial, observational, preclinical, or anecdotal/common use.

Be extremely literal. Do not infer benefits from mechanistic or animal findings. Do not generalize from diseased populations to healthy humans. For every study, include title, year, DOI/PMID if available, URL, study type, population/species, n, protocol, most notable results, adverse events, and a one-sentence limitation. Flag whether evidence is human, animal, cell, review, regulatory, or anecdotal.

Preferred output table columns:
peptide | evidence_type | study_title | year | doi | pmid | url | study_type | population_or_model | disease_context | n | age_or_species_details | route | dose_or_protocol | duration | endpoints | notable_results_literal | adverse_events_literal | limitations | mechanism_targets | genes | proteins | receptors | channels_transporters | cytokines_interleukins | clinical_use_context | enhancement_or_common_use_context | confidence | retrieved_date

If too many results appear, prioritize: FDA/regulatory labels first, phase 2/3 human trials second, randomized human trials third, peer-reviewed case reports fourth, animal studies fifth, mechanistic cell studies sixth, reviews only as background.

After exporting, preserve the Consensus export filename and retrieval date. Do not rewrite citations manually unless export is unavailable.
